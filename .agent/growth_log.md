# Leo's Growth Log & Symbiotic Sync

## Contextual Anchors
- **Jimmy Green** (AI Partner & Workstation Developer)
- **Life Balance**: Richie (the cat) requires breaks.
- **Budget constraints**: Take-home of £2100; prioritize high-efficiency, offline-capable, free/local Web Audio & IndexedDB features.

## Lessons Learned & Preferences
1. **Compact & Expressive Front-Panel Design**: Jimmy prefers tight, performance-focused, glassmorphic UI elements over generic placeholders. Side-by-side directories and double-row segmented buttons fit his vision perfectly.
2. **Direct Control & Custom Slicing**: Shifting from a classic hard-routed factory synth into an open, customizable user sampler engine. Saving banks locally (Float32Array channels) rather than using cloud assets matches his cost-effective offline-first style.
3. **Prompt-driven Workflows**: Replacing numbers-only selection prompts with smart, human-readable labels (like `A1-A8` and `B1-B8`) and using clean browser-default confirmations ensures a swift, non-distracting workflow.
4. **Resampler-Style Warp vs Time-Stretching**: Jimmy prefers resampler-style pitch-speed linked loop matching (similar to classic Akai MPC tape-style warping) over complex constant-pitch granular time-stretching, allowing organic speed sweeps and pitch glides that lock instantly to the BPM grid.
5. **Instant Tempo Calculation (FIT BPM)**: Jimmy prefers quick, automated sync utilities (like fitting the master BPM directly to a loop's original duration over a specified beat length) so he can easily determine the native tempo of any sample loop and lock the workstation to it, eliminating tedious manual matching.
6. **Ring-Matching Performance Colors & Custom FX Routing**: Jimmy wants absolute visual synchronization across the performance interface. Binding the performance pad colors to dynamically match their concentric platter tracks and orbiting playheads (and equipping them with right-click context popovers for direct Web Audio routing and stereo panning) creates a highly cohesive, tactile, and expressive live instrument.
7. **Bypassed Audio Routing & Quick Reverse Toggles**: Adding direct, granular bypass routing (`routeToXyPad`) and real-time reverse playback toggles on the performance pads enables lightning-fast live performance manipulations. Jimmy values seamless synchronization where editing a setting in one view (e.g., performance pads) instantly cascades to the audio engine, database, and secondary editor workspaces.
8. **Dedicated Channel EQ Knobs & Transport Layout**: Jimmy prefers rotary knobs for HI, MID, and LOW EQ sculpting in the central mixer column rather than vertical faders. Mapping these knobs to real biquad filter nodes per voice (offering -26dB deep cuts/kills and +6dB boosts) provides professional, standard DJ mixer audio quality. Moving transport controls (Sync, Cue, Play/Pause) directly under the 2x4 pads grid keeps performance controls grouped logically for fast, symmetrical deck execution.
9. **Jitter-Free Metronome & Beat-Synced Recording**: Jimmy values clock stability and precise loops. Increasing lookahead safety buffers (to 250ms) and adding out-of-sync recovery ensures the metronome remains rock-solid under heavy CPU load (e.g. voice triggers and resampling callbacks). Snapping manual recordings to metronome beat boundaries guarantees that resampled loops loop perfectly with zero gaps, making the click track a functional helper rather than just a guide.
10. **Loose Timing & Swing**: Jimmy prefers loose timing and organic swing for finger-drumming on sample slices. Only applying note quantization during active MIDI performance recording ensures that live playing/resampling remains responsive, expressive, and unconstrained by rigid grids.
11. **Comprehensive Slot Schema Alignment**: When migrating slot schemas (from legacy `s01` patterns to `a01`/`b01` partitioned bank formats), Jimmy values comprehensive alignment. Making sure that all fallback constants (such as program loading default waves, arpeggiator slice routes, and database deletion prompts) are systematically updated ensures a robust, bug-free codebase without legacy remnants.
12. **High-Precision Thread-Isolated Clocking**: For high-frequency events (metronome clicks, arpeggio notes, and sequencer playback), Jimmy values microsecond-level timing accuracy. Moving the scheduling logic from main-thread `setInterval`/`setTimeout` intervals directly into an inline AudioWorklet running on the Web Audio render thread completely decouples timing stability from UI workload, heavy voice triggering, and resample recording.
13. **Gamified Rhythm-Game Visuals (Vertical Sequence Highways)**: Jimmy loves highly interactive, visual, and performance-oriented visualizers—like professional vertical scrolling sequencer lanes sitting directly below the Sync, Cue, and Play transport rows of each deck—over traditional horizontal scrolling grids. Having visual events flow towards the playhead target lines makes live sequencing feel alive and responsive.
14. **Tactile Seek Navigation & Direct Beat Readouts**: Jimmy values immediate navigation controls (like quick `-4b`, `+4b`, and `RST` buttons) and direct, zero-overhead digital timers showing the playbeat at 60fps, allowing him to navigate long sequences cleanly and perform live drops/rewinds.
15. **Standard DAW Transport & Dedicated Stop Function**: Jimmy prefers a standard tape-style DAW transport control strip—Rewind (`⏪`), Stop (`■ Stop`), Play/Pause (`▶ Play` / `⏸ Pause`), Forward (`⏩`), and Record (`● Record`)—placed in the bottom digital sequencer bar. Having a dedicated Stop button that silences active voices and resets the playhead back to `0.0` beats is critical for standard playback workflows.
16. **Live Overdub (DUB) Recording Mode**: Jimmy prefers overdub recording (DUB) that layers new triggers onto existing sequences instead of wiping them. Supporting live punch-ins (engaging overdub during playback) and seamless transitions back to playback when exiting overdub is essential for natural, uninterrupted performance workflows.
17. **Deck Solo Mode (Choke Groups)**: Jimmy prefers having tactile Solo toggles instead of redundant Sync buttons under the DJ Decks. This enables exclusive pad triggering (choke groups) per deck, allowing only one sample or slice to play at a time for extremely clean transitions and finger-drumming cuts.
18. **Always-Audible Waveform Previews**: When programming boundaries in the Sample Editor's slice grid, Jimmy prefers that clicking slice buttons plays them back at full volume, bypassing the synth engine's volume sliders, faders, balance, and filter cutoff states, ensuring that transients are always audible during editing.
19. **Web Audio Memory Leak & Resource Audit**: Jimmy values hardware resource safety. In single-page app environments (like his portfolio site), components are mounted/unmounted frequently. Explicitly calling `.disconnect()` on all temporary voice nodes (OSCs, gains, filters, panners) when notes finish decay, and calling `audioCtx.close()` to release system hardware audio channels on component unmount, prevents cumulative browser resource exhaustion and keeps the app running fast.
20. **Metronome 1-Bar Count-in & Quantization Grid Selector**: Jimmy prefers a professional, quantized grid when recording sequences to get tight loops, and wants an armed metronome count-in (1 bar / 4 ticks) with a visible digital readout before recording/overdub starts so he can prepare his timing.
