## Lessons Learned & Preferences
1. **Compact & Expressive Front-Panel Design**: Jimmy prefers tight, performance-focused, glassmorphic UI elements over generic placeholders. Side-by-side directories and double-row segmented buttons fit his vision perfectly.
2. **Direct Control & Custom Slicing**: Shifting from a classic hard-routed factory synth into an open, customizable user sampler engine. Saving banks locally (Float32Array channels) rather than using cloud assets matches his cost-effective offline-first style.
3. **Prompt-driven Workflows**: Replacing numbers-only selection prompts with smart, human-readable labels (like `A1-A8` and `B1-B8`) and using clean browser-default confirmations ensures a swift, non-distracting workflow.
4. **Resampler-Style Warp vs Time-Stretching**: Jimmy prefers resampler-style pitch-speed linked loop matching (similar to classic Akai MPC tape-style warping) over complex constant-pitch granular time-stretching, allowing organic speed sweeps and pitch glides that lock instantly to the BPM grid.
